What ValuCast is

ValuCast turns player projections into values tuned to your league's categories, weights, and roster rules. You can value players from two sources: Steamer (an external comparison board, and the default) or ValuCast H+P, our own in-house projection.

The two boards

The default board values current-season actuals + Steamer rest-of-season. The ValuCast board values our own full-season projection. Flipping the toggle is useful for eyeballing differences, but it is not an apples-to-apples backtest; our real check is the held-out validation below.

How the model works

For each player we weight recent seasons, regress toward the league average (more for small samples), and adjust for age. For hitters we first de-noise the inputs toward Statcast expected stats (Savant xBA/xSLG) before projecting. For pitchers we project per batter faced and blend a pitcher's starting-vs-relieving usage continuously rather than forcing a starter/reliever cliff.

Under the hood

A Marcel-style method: recent seasons weighted {{ hit_weights }} (most recent first), regressed to the league mean, age-adjusted, with per-opportunity rate components reconstructed. Hitters add Statcast input de-noising (blend actual contact/power toward Savant xBA/xSLG, redistributing into 1B/2B/3B/HR by the player's own extra-base mix). Pitchers use a continuous starter-probability blend so swingmen and converted arms aren't mis-categorised. The pitching model consumes no third-party projection.

Model equations

Season weighting of a per-opportunity rate r over the three prior seasons: weights {{ hit_weights }} → r̄ = (5·r₋₁ + 4·r₋₂ + 3·r₋₃) / (5·PA₋₁ + 4·PA₋₂ + 3·PA₋₃).

Regression to the mean with a regression constant n_reg (hitters {{ hit_n_reg }}, pitchers {{ pit_n_reg }}): projected = (observed·N + league·n_reg) / (N + n_reg).

Age adjustment nudges the projection along an empirical aging curve.

Hitter de-noising (knobs α): blend the actual rate toward the Savant expected rate, r* = (1−α)·r + α·x (x = xBA/xSLG), then redistribute the extra hits/bases preserving the player's hit-type mix.

Pitcher role blend: with psp the starter probability, each component is shifted continuously by f[c]^(h_sp − p_sp) — no hard SP/RP split.

Worked example. A hitter with AB-rate .270 / .280 / .260 over the last three years (weights 5/4/3) gives a weighted .267; regressing ~600 PA of signal toward a .250 league mean with n_reg = {{ hit_n_reg }} pulls it toward ~.261; an age nudge and Statcast de-noise toward the player's xBA give the final projected average.

What we have and haven't proven

Our validation is against internal baselines (persistence and classic Marcel), which it beats on held-out data. ValuCast has not yet proven it beats Steamer or ZiPS: we lack matching archived preseason projections for a fair, apples-to-apples historical backtest. So Steamer is an external comparison board, with a fair historical benchmark pending — not a benchmark we've beaten.
